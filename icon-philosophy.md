# Chromatic Taxonomy

A philosophy of precise categorical color as knowledge system. Information encoded not through shape alone, but through the deliberate assignment of chromatic identity to distinct domains — each hue a taxonomic marker, as rigorous as a scientific classification.

Space is divided with surgical intention. Each zone carries its own chromatic weight, bounded by invisible but felt constraints. The grid is not decoration — it is architecture. Negative space breathes between the fields, creating rhythm through absence as much as presence. Every margin is calculated; every gap deliberate.

Color families are assigned with the care of a master chemist mixing pigments: cool cerulean for language and document, verdant emerald for data and structure, vermillion for the irreducible and permanent, amber for the kinetic and presentational. These are not arbitrary — they are the product of deep cultural and perceptual research, refined through painstaking iteration.

Form is geometric and unambiguous. Rounded corners soften without compromising precision. Shadows are architectural — not decorative, but structural guides for the eye. Every radius, every stroke weight, every opacity value is chosen with the conviction of someone who has spent countless hours in pursuit of the single correct answer.

The whole must read at a whisper — legible at 16 pixels, resonant at 512. This demands meticulous hierarchy: the dominant shape commands, the secondary shapes support, the background recedes with discipline. The result is the work of a craftsman who knows that perfection is not addition but the removal of everything that does not belong.
